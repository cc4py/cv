Charles A. Chang, PhD

Biotech Scientist
Islet Cell & BMT Specialist
San Francisco Bay Area, California

I am a biomedical scientist and cell therapy manufacturing consultant currently working in the SF Bay Area.

My research experience has spanned from organic chemistry synthesis of vaccine adjuvants and antibody-drug conjugates, to chemical modification and reducing inflammation of transplanted living tissue, immune tolerance and treatment of autoimmune disease through bone marrow chimerism, and discovery of novel non-genotoxic bone marrow conditioning agents.

Having spent nearly a decade working in and around hospitals, I am comfortable interacting with clinicians in high-pressure mission-critical environments. I have also worked directly with Organ Procurement Organizations (OPOs) from initial protocol authoring, to being on-call for screening offers, to organ retrieval or receipt.

In addition to basic wet lab experience including cell sorting and high parameter (up to 18-color) flow cytometry, I have extensive hands-on experience in mouse models, porcine models, as well as non-human primate and human tissues, and the IACUC/IRB/USDA/USFWS regulations surrounding their usage. I am familiar with relevant Contract Research Organizations (CROs) and proficient in quickly establishing effective study protocols with relevant high-confidence methods.

I have a strong innate go-getter attitude which contributes to my success and the success of the team. I am patient, perceptive, and a good listener, making me an effective colleague and mentor. Important values to me include: humble competence, innovative & efficient problem solving, and energizing inspiration.

My professional goals are to use my strong technical skills and experience to advance curative treatments in the cell therapy and biologics spaces.
